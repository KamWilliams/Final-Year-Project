Longer vs Short Stories

When choosing a novel to begin reading there a few things to consider. Not only should you consider the genre and type of novel, you should also consider the length of the novel. A longer book will allow you to become engrosed in a book for much longer, but will take a lot longer to read and can be intimidating to newer readers. Shorter books will allow for quicker reads and will be less intimidating for newer readers.

A Deeper Dive

Into Longer Reads

The longer a book is the mroe the writer can fit into the book. This allows the writer to build their story a lot more than a shorter story would allow them to. Longer books will allow you as the reader to stay engrossed the book for a much longer period of time. Long novels are able to meet the demands of readers who grow increasingly accustomed to instantly accessing captivating and complex stories.

A Deeper Dive

Short Stories

Short stories have the abilty to capture your attention and develop the plot in a consise manor. Short isn't only in reference to the number of words or characters. It also means the fast pace, the single-minded plot, and the concise form. It’s not justified to say that we face the revival of short stories. Short stories may turn out to be the most effective tool of a revival of reading in digital times. As new readers can use these short stories to develop their intrest in reading again without having to try and force themselves to get through a longer book.
