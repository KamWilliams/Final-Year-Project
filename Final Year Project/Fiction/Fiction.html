Fiction vs Non-Fiction

Fiction vs Non-Fiction

Realism vs Imagination

In general, fiction refers to stories based on imagined plots, settings, and characters, whereas nonfiction refers to stories based on actual events and people.
Whether you would like to transported into a world of fantasy or read about real world events, there is something for everyone.

A Deeper Dive

Into Fiction

Fiction is made up and based solely on the author's imagination. Fiction includes short stories, novels, myths, legends, and fairy tales. While locales, story ideas, and characters in fiction are sometimes based on real-life events or individuals, writers use them as starting points for their stories. Stephen King, for example, sets several of his stories and novels in the imaginary town of Derry, Maine. Derry is not a real place, but it is modelled on King's hometown of Bangor. King has even designed a Derry topography that mirrors the actual topography of Bangor.

A Deeper Dive

Into Non-Fiction

Nonfiction, on the other hand, is true and reports on true events. Nonfiction includes histories, biographies, journalism, and essays. Nonfiction is usually held to a higher Standard than fiction. A few smatterings of fact in a work of fiction do not make it true, yet a few fabrications in a nonfiction work can render the story untrustworthy. However, nonfiction often uses many of the techniques of fiction to make it more appealing. In Cold Blood is widely regarded as one of the best works of nonfiction to significantly blur the line between fiction and nonfiction, since Capote’s descriptions and detailing of events are so rich and evocative. However, this has led to questions about the veracity of his account.
